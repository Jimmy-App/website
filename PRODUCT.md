# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Independent fitness coaches in modern disciplines: Hyrox, CrossFit, functional fitness, athletic conditioning. Typically solo operators or small teams with 10–200 clients. They are hands-on practitioners, not business admins. They use tools on their phones between sessions, late at night, or during travel. They are skeptical of software that promises too much; they trust what they can see working.

## Product Purpose

Jimmy is a retention platform for modern fitness coaches. It replaces the scattered stack of WhatsApp, Excel, PDFs, and fragmented tools with a single native iOS/Android app: structured workout programming, a Skool-style community feed, 1:1 messaging, Stripe payments, and a course builder. The goal is not to manage clients — it is to make them belong. Success looks like a coach who spends more time coaching and less time managing, with clients who stay longer because the experience is worth staying for.

## Brand Personality

Premium, Modern, Focused. Direct coach-to-coach voice. No corporate speak, no hedging, no SaaS jargon. Confident without being loud. The tone is a senior coach talking to a peer: specific, credible, zero fluff.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS minimalism (Notion, Linear, Loom style): cold gray palette, tiny type, tool-as-neutral-utility aesthetic. Jimmy is a brand, not a utility.
- Bodybuilding/bro fitness apps: neon accents, black backgrounds, hyper-aggressive tone. Jimmy is premium and modern, not gym-mirror.
- Cream/warm-neutral AI-default landing pages: the warm-sand-on-white aesthetic is the saturated AI reflex. Jimmy's warmth comes from the purple brand and the copy, not from a tinted background.
- Endless eyebrow-labeled sections, numbered scaffolding (01/02/03), identical icon-card grids.

## Design Principles

1. **Show belonging, not features.** Every section should evoke the feeling of a tight coach community, not a feature checklist. Specifics beat promises.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** The brand is premium because it knows what it is and doesn't try to impress everything at once. One strong visual moment per section beats five medium ones.
3. **Coach-first perspective.** Copy and layout speak to a coach's day, not an investor's deck. If it sounds like a pitch deck, rewrite it.
4. **The product is the proof.** Where possible, show the app doing real things: real workout blocks, real community feed, real payment flows. Don't describe; demonstrate.
5. **Purple owns the brand.** The purple (#8A32E0) is not an accent — it is the identity. It should appear with intention, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1. Large text / bold UI ≥ 3:1. All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Multilingual: EN, FR, ES (via next-intl). No content gated behind motion.
